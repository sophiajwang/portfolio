4D Printing Manufacturing Approaches Programmed actuation of liquid crystal elastomers (LCE) Ozgun Kilic Afsar and Sophia Wang
Specialized machine design for pulling and printing liquid crystal elastomers before curing for photosensitivity

Our machine has two modes of operation: pulling and direct "ink" write extrusion. The pulling mode enables long, "infinite" threads of LCE thanks to a continuous rotation and collection spool. Applications of the thread include knitting for advanced dynamics. The direct write mode allows for custom shapes and patterns to emerge with print path orientation. All extrusions must be continuously heated, with the entire machine blocked from UV light. The advantage of LCE is its application to soft robotics, producing a new material for actuation that has historically been stiff. Potential contexts for LCEs are artificial muscles and wearable devices that mold to the user.
